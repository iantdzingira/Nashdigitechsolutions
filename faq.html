Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers about our services, process, and pricing.
What services does Nash Digitech offer?

We offer a full range of digital solutions: website design & development, mobile apps (iOS/Android), custom software systems, AI & machine learning, branding & UI/UX design, and digital marketing. We also provide tech training for individuals and teams.

How much does a website cost?

Our website design starts at $500 for a basic informational site. Complex e‑commerce or custom web applications range from $1,000 to $10,000+. Every project is unique – we’ll provide a detailed quote after understanding your requirements.

How long does it take to build a website?

A standard brochure website typically takes 2–4 weeks. More complex projects (e‑commerce, custom platforms) can take 6–12 weeks, depending on scope and feedback speed. We’ll give you a timeline estimate before starting.

Do you require a deposit?

Yes, we ask for a 50% non‑refundable deposit to begin work. The remaining balance is due upon completion and before final delivery. This is standard practice in the industry and secures your slot in our schedule.

Will I own the source code / design?

Absolutely. After full payment, you own the final deliverables (code, design files). We only retain the right to showcase the work in our portfolio unless we agree otherwise.

Do you offer ongoing maintenance and support?

Yes. We offer monthly maintenance packages (starting at $100/month) that include security updates, backups, content changes, and priority support. You can also request one‑off fixes as needed.

What technologies do you specialize in?

We’re fluent in modern stacks: HTML5/CSS3/JavaScript, React, Vue, Node.js, Python (Django/Flask), PHP (Laravel), WordPress, Shopify, and mobile with Flutter / React Native. For AI/ML we use TensorFlow, PyTorch, and cloud platforms.

Are you based in Zimbabwe? Do you work with international clients?

Yes, our headquarters are in Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe. We happily serve clients worldwide – most communication is via email, WhatsApp, and video calls. Time zone differences are managed flexibly.
